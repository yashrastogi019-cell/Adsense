Best Project Management Software for Remote Teams

RA
Rasto
Published Apr 22, 2025

Why Remote Teams Need Dedicated PM Software

Remote work has shifted from a temporary arrangement to a permanent reality for millions of teams worldwide. With team members spread across time zones, the right project management software becomes the central nervous system of your organization — the single source of truth where work gets planned, tracked, and delivered.

We evaluated five of the most popular project management platforms across real remote team scenarios: sprint planning, cross-team collaboration, resource management, and async communication. Here is what we found.

1. Asana — Best for Marketing and Operations Teams

Asana excels at managing complex, multi-step workflows with its intuitive project views and automation features. The Timeline view provides Gantt-chart functionality that makes it easy to spot dependencies and potential bottlenecks. For marketing teams managing campaigns across multiple channels, Asana's workflow automations can save hours of manual status updates and task routing each week.

The free tier supports up to 10 users with basic features, making it accessible for small teams. The Premium plan at $10.99 per user per month adds Timeline, custom fields, and advanced search — features most growing teams will eventually need. The biggest drawback is that Asana can feel overwhelming for simple projects due to its feature density.

2. Monday.com — Best Visual Interface

Monday.com stands out with the most visually appealing and intuitive interface of any PM tool we tested. The color-coded boards, drag-and-drop functionality, and visual dashboards make project status immediately clear at a glance. For teams that are visual thinkers or managing client-facing projects, Monday.com makes it easy to create polished views that everyone can understand.

Monday.com also offers robust automation capabilities and integrations with over 200 third-party tools. The platform has expanded beyond pure project management to include CRM, dev tools, and marketing management — making it a potential all-in-one platform for small businesses. Pricing starts at $8 per user per month for the Basic plan.

3. ClickUp — Best Feature-to-Price Ratio

ClickUp has positioned itself as the most feature-rich PM tool at the most competitive price point. The free tier is exceptionally generous, offering unlimited tasks, members, and 100MB of storage. The Unlimited plan at just $7 per user per month adds unlimited storage, integrations, dashboards, and Gantt charts — features that competitors charge significantly more for.

The trade-off is complexity. ClickUp has so many features that new users often feel overwhelmed during onboarding. The platform has improved significantly in terms of performance and reliability, but it can still feel slower than Asana or Monday.com on complex projects. For teams willing to invest time in setup, ClickUp offers exceptional value.

4. Notion — Best for Documentation-Heavy Teams

Notion occupies a unique space between project management and knowledge management. While it is not a traditional PM tool, its flexible database system, wiki-style documentation, and customizable views make it an excellent choice for teams where documentation is as important as task tracking. Development teams, content teams, and agencies often find that Notion's all-in-one approach eliminates the need for separate documentation and PM tools.

The free tier is generous for individual use, and the Plus plan at $8 per member per month adds unlimited file uploads and 30-day page history. Notion's AI features also add value for content-focused teams, offering in-line writing assistance and automated summaries.

Our Recommendation

For most remote teams, ClickUp offers the best overall value with its generous free tier and competitive pricing. For teams that prioritize aesthetics and ease of use, Monday.com delivers the most polished experience. For workflow-heavy operations, Asana remains the gold standard. And for teams that need tight integration between documentation and project management, Notion is the smartest choice.
