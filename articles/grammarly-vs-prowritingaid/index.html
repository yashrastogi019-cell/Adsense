Grammarly vs ProWritingAid: Which Writing Assistant Is Better?

RA
Rasto
Published Apr 10, 2025

The Writing Assistant Showdown

Grammar checkers have evolved far beyond simple spell-check. Modern writing assistants analyze your text for grammar, style, tone, readability, and even plagiarism. The two dominant players — Grammarly and ProWritingAid — take different approaches to helping you write better. We tested both extensively across blog posts, business emails, academic papers, and fiction to determine which one delivers more value.

Grammarly — The Polished All-Rounder

Grammarly has the most polished user experience of any writing tool. The browser extension works seamlessly across Gmail, Google Docs, social media, and virtually any text field on the web. Suggestions appear in real-time with clear explanations of why a change is recommended, making it both a correction tool and a learning tool.

The free tier catches basic grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors — covering the needs of casual writers. Grammarly Premium at $12 per month adds advanced suggestions for clarity, engagement, delivery tone, and word choice. The GrammarlyGO AI assistant can rewrite entire paragraphs, adjust tone, and generate text from prompts.

Grammarly's biggest strength is its consistency and reliability. It rarely produces false positives, and its suggestions are almost always improvements. The tone detection feature is particularly useful for professional communication — it can tell you if your email sounds too aggressive, too passive, or just right.

ProWritingAid — The Writer's Workshop

ProWritingAid takes a deeper, more analytical approach to writing improvement. While it catches grammar and spelling errors just as well as Grammarly, its real value lies in its detailed writing reports. The platform analyzes your text for overused words, sentence length variation, readability scores, pacing, dialogue tags, clichés, and dozens of other stylistic metrics.

For serious writers — novelists, bloggers, content marketers — these in-depth reports are invaluable. The Style Report highlights vague, redundant, or unclear writing. The Overused Words report catches repeated phrases you might not notice. The Readability Report ensures your content matches your target audience's reading level. No other tool provides this depth of analysis.

ProWritingAid's pricing is also significantly more competitive. A yearly subscription costs $79, compared to Grammarly's $144 per year. ProWritingAid also offers a lifetime license for a one-time payment of $399 — making it dramatically cheaper over the long term.

Our Verdict

For everyday writing — emails, social media, quick documents — Grammarly is the better choice. Its real-time suggestions are faster, more polished, and require less effort. For serious long-form writing — blog posts, articles, books, academic papers — ProWritingAid delivers far more value with its in-depth analysis reports and better pricing. Many professional writers use both: Grammarly for daily communication and ProWritingAid for editing major pieces.
